Concept of the Industrial Revolution 4.0



Industrial Revolution 4.0



The term "fourth industrial revolution" has been applied to important technological developments several times over the past 75 years, and is for academic discussion. Industrial 4.0 concept or intelligent plant was first introduced at the Hannover Industrial Fair in the Federal Republic of Germany in 2011. Industry 4.0 aims to intelligently process manufacturing and management in the industry. make. The launch of the 4.0 Industry in Germany has pushed other advanced countries such as the United States, Japan, China and India to accelerate the development of similar programs in order to maintain their competitive edge.



In 2013, a new keyword "Industry 4.0" (Industrie 4.0) began to emerge from a German government report referring to this phrase in terms of high technology, production without human participation.

Industry 4.0 is the current trend in automation and data exchange in manufacturing technology. It includes real system-virtual cyberspace ( cyber-physical system ), Internet things and cloud computing and computational cognitive ( Cognitive computing )

LOOKING BACK ON THE INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION IN HISTORY

Klaus Schwab, founder and president of the World Economic Forum, gives a simpler view of the Industrial Revolution 4.0:

"The first industrial revolution utilized water and steam power to mechanize production." The second revolution took place through the use of electricity for mass production. Information technology to automate production Now, the Fourth Industrial Revolution is flourishing from the third revolution that combines technologies together, blurring the boundaries between physics, engineering digit and biology.


PRINCIPLE

There are 4 design principles in industry 4.0. These principles assist companies in defining and implementing the perspectives of industry 4.0

  • Interoperability: The ability to communicate and connect machines, devices, sensors and people connect and communicate with one another through a network of people connected to the internet or a network of thousands of people connected to the internet.
  • Transparency: The ability of information systems to create a virtual world of reality by enriching digital factory models with sensor data. This requires the aggregation of raw sensor data to higher value context information.
  • Supporting Technologies: First of all the capabilities of human support systems by gathering and envisioning information universally for making informed decisions and addressing emergencies. short notes. Secondly, the ability of cyberspace-physics systems to assist people in performing tasks that are unpleasant, too much energy or unsafe for humans.
  • Decentral decision-making: Virtual-cyber space systems have the power to make their own decisions and perform tasks as automatically as possible. Only in exceptional circumstances, interferences, or goals Conflicts with each other will be delegated to higher levels.
